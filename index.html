About Us!

The Association of Black Social Workers Child Adoption Counseling and Referral Services (aka ABSW Adoption Services) is a not-for-profit adoption agency that is authorized/licensed by the New York State Office of Children and Family Services. The Service began in 1975 and has over been serving the community for over 40 years. It is a program affiliated with the New York Chapter Association of Black Social Workers but has its own separate incorporation.


The Agency works with couples and single persons who wish to adopt children that are free for adoption who are in the foster care system and families who wish to engage in domestic private adoptions. We also do intercountry adoptions for families who wish to adopt minor relatives. We are not a foster care agency but work with agencies throughout New York City and surrounding counties as well as nationwide who do foster care. The Service works with Hague Accredited agencies to do intercountry relative adoptions.

Adoption Requirements

In compliance with New York State laws, adoptive parents must be 18 years of age or older. They may be married, divorced, legally separated or single men and women. If a married person wishes to adopt as a single person but is not legally separated, he/she must have been living apart from their spouse for at least 3 years. Intercountry adoptions might have different requirements depending upon the laws of the particular country.


The major requirements are the ability to love and make a permanent commitment to a child, have adequate space in the home to include sleeping arrangements for another child(ren). Adequate child care arrangements if parent(s) work and a source of income.


You do NOT have to own your own home or have a lot of money.

How Our Service Operates

Recruitment

Prospective adoptive parents are recruited by general and targeted recruitment methods using traditional and social media; speaking engagements before community groups, religious organizations, conferences and parent organizations; distributing flyers and brochures and posters, participating in community and citywide fairs, etc.

Orientation Meetings

Group Orientation meetings are held once a month or more often when needed. Individual orientation sessions may also be held. These meetings provide an overview and inform prospective parents as to:

    The skills needed, duties, rights and responsibilities of adoptive/foster parents
    The needs and description of the children and families served by the foster care system
    The similarities and differences in becoming a parent by birth vs. adoption
    The policies and procedures of ABSW Adoption Services and other child care agencies
    The difference between being a foster parent and an adoptive parent and the roles of each
    Life-long issues experienced by adoptees and how adoptive parents can be of help
    The steps to be taken to become approved as an adoptive parent

Adoption Training

Our Service will conduct GPSII Model Approach to Partnership in Parenting (MAPP) to prospective adoptive parents. Each group training series will be 30 hours of training given for 10 weeks, three hours per week on week nights or weekends. The MAPP training will give insight as to the separation and loss issues as well as the sociological, psychological and behavioral issues experienced and displayed by children in care and how to handle such issues.

Home Studies

Prospective adoptive parents that complete adoption training and who complete and submit our Agency Application, will be evaluated via a home study which assesses the applicants’ readiness to become adoptive parents. Home Studies will consist of:

    TAn office visit and at least two home visits in which prospective single parents or couples and members of their households will be interviewed and evaluated/assessed as to their readiness and understanding regarding adding adoptive children to their household and how their lives will change. Additional interviews may be conducted when necessary.
    Couples will be interviewed together and separately. Their backgrounds and current functioning, financial status, health and relationships, their understanding of child development, separation and loss issues and acting-out behavior on the part of children will be assessed
    Comprehensive Medical exams will be required from all persons in the household 14 years of age and older. A Medical Statement from a physician will be required as to the health status of children in the household under fourteen years of age.
    Financial copies of documents such as proof of income (i.e. pay stubs or letters from employers) income tax 1040 and w-2 forms and other documents that prove that the family is able to care for itself and additional adopted children, will be collected. Copies of rent/mortgage, utility bills and other proof of residence will be collected and a financial statement of assets and liabilities will be required
    The physical home will be assessed for safety issues, sleeping arrangements and smoke and carbon monoxide detectors
    References will be collected
    A case record will be established containing information on the family
    Statements regarding criminal behavior, substance abuse, and mental health history will be asked of applicants and adults in the household 18 years of age and older.
    Criminal History checks via fingerprinting will be done by the New York State Division of Criminal Justice on all persons in the household eighteen (18) years of age and older
    Child Abuse clearances will be conducted via the New York State Central Registry (SCR)
    A check of the New York State Justice Center for the Protection of People with Special Needs Request for Staff Exclusion List (SEL) will be conducted on each applicant and all persons in the household 18 years of age and older.
    Approved families will be certified as adoptive parents.
