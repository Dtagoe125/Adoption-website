About Us!

The Association of Black Social Workers Child Adoption Counseling and Referral Services (aka ABSW Adoption Services) is a not-for-profit adoption agency that is authorized/licensed by the New York State Office of Children and Family Services. The Service began in 1975 and has over been serving the community for over 40 years. It is a program affiliated with the New York Chapter Association of Black Social Workers but has its own separate incorporation.


The Agency works with couples and single persons who wish to adopt children that are free for adoption who are in the foster care system and families who wish to engage in domestic private adoptions. We also do intercountry adoptions for families who wish to adopt minor relatives. We are not a foster care agency but work with agencies throughout New York City and surrounding counties as well as nationwide who do foster care. The Service works with Hague Accredited agencies to do intercountry relative adoptions.

Adoption Requirements

In compliance with New York State laws, adoptive parents must be 18 years of age or older. They may be married, divorced, legally separated or single men and women. If a married person wishes to adopt as a single person but is not legally separated, he/she must have been living apart from their spouse for at least 3 years. Intercountry adoptions might have different requirements depending upon the laws of the particular country.


The major requirements are the ability to love and make a permanent commitment to a child, have adequate space in the home to include sleeping arrangements for another child(ren). Adequate child care arrangements if parent(s) work and a source of income.


You do NOT have to own your own home or have a lot of money.
